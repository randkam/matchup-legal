Terms of Service

Last Updated: March 8, 2026

Welcome to MatchUp. These Terms of Service ("Terms") govern your access to and use of the MatchUp mobile application and related services (collectively, the "Service").

By creating an account or using MatchUp, you agree to be bound by these Terms.

If you do not agree to these Terms, you may not use the Service.

1. Description of the Service

MatchUp is a platform that allows users to create teams and register those teams for organized sports tournaments and activities.

MatchUp facilitates tournament organization but does not act as a sports league, referee organization, or athletic governing body. MatchUp may organize events directly or in partnership with organizers.

Participation in tournaments is voluntary, and users participate at their own risk.

2. User Accounts

To use certain features of the Service, users must create an account.

Users agree to:

  • Provide accurate and complete information when creating an account
  • Maintain the security of their login credentials
  • Be responsible for all activity that occurs under their account

MatchUp reserves the right to suspend or terminate accounts that violate these Terms.

3. Teams and Tournament Registration

MatchUp tournaments operate on a team-based registration system.

Users must first create a team within the application before registering for a tournament.

Each team must designate a team captain.

The team captain is responsible for:

  • Registering the team for tournaments
  • Paying any required entry fees or deposits
  • Managing the team roster before tournament deadlines

Teams may register for tournaments regardless of the number of players currently on their roster. However, teams must meet the minimum player requirement at game time in order to participate.

Teams are responsible for ensuring they have enough players present to compete.

4. Player Eligibility and Name Verification

Only players listed on a team’s official roster within the MatchUp application may participate in tournament games.

Players may be asked to confirm their first and last name during tournament check-in to verify their eligibility.

Tournament organizers may verify player names against the team roster recorded in the MatchUp application.

If a team is found to be using a player who is not listed on the official roster, MatchUp reserves the right to:

  • Record the game as a forfeit
  • Disqualify the team from the tournament
  • Withhold any applicable deposits or entry fees

MatchUp reserves the right to determine player eligibility.

5. Tournament Registration Deadline

Teams may register for or unregister from a tournament up to forty-eight (48) hours before the tournament start time.

Teams that unregister before the 48-hour deadline will receive refunds for any applicable entry fees or deposits.

Once the 48-hour deadline has passed:

  • Tournament registration closes
  • Teams may no longer unregister
  • Entry fees and deposits become non-refundable

6. Tournament Brackets and Rosters

Approximately 48 hours before the tournament begins, MatchUp will generate the official tournament bracket and game schedule.

After the bracket has been generated:

  • Teams may not withdraw from the tournament
  • Team rosters become locked
  • Game schedules will be published within the application

Teams are responsible for reviewing their game schedule and arriving on time for their games.

7. Tournament Deposits

Certain tournaments may require a team deposit to secure participation.

Deposit policies are as follows:

  • Deposits are refundable if a team unregisters before the 48-hour registration deadline
  • Deposits become non-refundable after the 48-hour deadline

If the team participates in the tournament:

  • The deposit will be refunded immediately after the team arrives on time to their first scheduled game

If the team fails to meet the minimum required players at the start time of their first game:

  • The game will be forfeited
  • The deposit will not be refunded

Example:

For a 3v3 tournament, if fewer than three players are present at the scheduled start time of the first game, the team will be considered absent and the deposit will be forfeited.

8. Tournament Entry Fees

Some tournaments may require an entry fee.

Entry fee policies:

  • Entry fees may be fully refunded if a team unregisters before the 48-hour registration deadline
  • Entry fees are non-refundable after the 48-hour deadline

Entry fees and deposits must be paid by the team captain when registering the team.

9. Game Start Times and Late Arrival

Teams are expected to arrive before their scheduled game time.

A maximum grace period of five (5) minutes may be allowed after the scheduled start time of a game.

If a team does not have the required number of players present within five (5) minutes of the scheduled game time:

  • The game may be recorded as a forfeit
  • The opposing team will automatically advance in the tournament
  • The team may lose their deposit, if applicable

MatchUp reserves the right to adjust game start times or apply forfeits as necessary to maintain the tournament schedule.

10. Prize Payouts

Prize payouts, if applicable, will be awarded to the winning team following the completion of the tournament.

Prize payments will be distributed as follows:

  • The prize amount will be sent via Interac e-Transfer to the team captain of the winning team after the final game of the tournament is completed
  • The team captain must provide a valid email address capable of receiving Interac e-Transfers

Prize payouts are issued based on the official tournament results recorded by MatchUp.

By participating in a tournament, team members acknowledge that prize payouts will be issued only to the team captain. MatchUp is not responsible for distributing prize winnings among team members.

11. Tournament Decisions and Disputes

MatchUp reserves the right to make final decisions regarding tournament organization, scheduling, results, and rule interpretations.

In the event of disputes between teams or players, MatchUp or the designated event organizer will determine the outcome of the dispute.

All tournament results and prize payouts determined by MatchUp are final.

By participating in a MatchUp tournament, users agree to accept the final decisions of MatchUp regarding tournament outcomes and prize distribution.

12. User Conduct

Users agree not to:

  • Attempt to manipulate tournament results
  • Provide false information during registration
  • Interfere with the normal operation of the Service

MatchUp reserves the right to remove users from tournaments or suspend accounts for violations.

By registering for and participating in tournaments organized through MatchUp, users acknowledge and accept these risks.

Users agree that MatchUp, its operators, and affiliates are not responsible for injuries, damages, or losses that occur during participation in events.
